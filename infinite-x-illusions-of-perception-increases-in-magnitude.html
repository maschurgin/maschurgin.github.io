Infinite X: Illusions Of Perceptual Increases In Magnitude

Previous demonstrations of two-frame animations, using either a blank frame or a series of polarity-inverted frames, have been shown to create a sense of perpetual motion. My work has shown that this phenomenon can be extended to more complex domains, including size and emotion. First is a demonstration of  a bar graph whose bars appear to be perpetually increasing or decreasing in size:
However, it is also possible to adapt this phenomenon beyond size to more complex domains, such as emotion. Here is an example of animated faces that appear to be expressing perpetually increasing anger, although this effect can also be applied to other emotions, such as happiness. 

It is also worth noting that when the animation is paused, the observer experiences a robust after-effect of reversed emotion.
This work was presented as a demonstration in 2011 at the Visual Sciences Society (VSS) annual meeting.
